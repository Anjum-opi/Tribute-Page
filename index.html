Barack Obama

The 44th President of the United States

Barack Obama, the first African-American President of the United States, served two terms from 2009 to 2017.

Here's a timeline of Barack Obama's life:

  • 1961 - Born in Honolulu, Hawaii
  • 1983 - Graduates from Columbia University with a degree in Political Science
  • 1988 - Enrolls at Harvard Law School and becomes the first African-American president of the *Harvard Law Review*
  • 1992 - Marries Michelle Robinson, whom he met while working at a law firm
  • 1996 - Elected to the Illinois State Senate, representing the 13th district
  • 2004 - Wins the U.S. Senate seat for Illinois and gains national attention with a keynote address at the Democratic National Convention
  • 2008 - Elected the 44th President of the United States, defeating John McCain
  • 2009 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts to strengthen international diplomacy
  • 2010 - Signs the Affordable Care Act (Obamacare) into law
  • 2012 - Reelected as President, defeating Mitt Romney
  • 2017 - Concludes his presidency and delivers a farewell address emphasizing democracy and unity
  • 2020 - Releases his memoir *A Promised Land*
